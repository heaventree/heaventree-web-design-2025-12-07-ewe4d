Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how heaventree Web design (“heaventree”, “we”, “us”, or “our”) collects, uses, and protects your personal information when you visit our website, use our services, or interact with us in any other way. We are committed to safeguarding your privacy and handling your data in a transparent, secure, and lawful manner.

Last updated: 7 December 2025

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, store, share, and protect information that relates to an identified or identifiable individual (“Personal Data”) when you use the heaventree Web design website, interact with our template marketplace, or otherwise communicate with us.

By accessing or using our website or services, you agree to the terms of this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with this policy, please do not use our website or submit any Personal Data to us.

Depending on where you are located, we process your Personal Data in accordance with applicable data protection laws, including the EU General Data Protection Regulation (“GDPR”) where relevant.

2. Information We Collect

We collect information in three main ways: information you provide directly, information collected automatically, and information from third parties.

Information you provide

We collect Personal Data that you voluntarily provide when you:

  • Contact us by email or through forms
  • Request information about templates or pricing
  • Subscribe to updates, newsletters, or marketing
  • Provide content or assets for your website project

Information collected automatically

When you visit our website, we may automatically collect certain information, such as:

  • IP address and approximate location
  • Browser type and device information
  • Pages viewed and referring URLs
  • Date, time, and duration of visits

Information from third parties

We may receive information about you from:

  • Payment or billing providers
  • Analytics and advertising partners
  • Publicly available sources (e.g. websites)

Types of Personal Data

Depending on how you interact with us, the Personal Data we collect may include:

  • Contact details such as your name, email address, phone number, and company name.
  • Business information such as your industry, website URL, and project requirements.
  • Usage data such as interactions with our pages, template previews, and navigation patterns.
  • Technical data such as IP address, device identifiers, browser type, and operating system.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes, based on our legitimate interests, your consent, or to fulfill a contract with you:

  • To provide, operate, and maintain our website and services.
  • To respond to your inquiries, support requests, and project briefs.
  • To prepare proposals, quotes, or agreements related to your project.
  • To configure, launch, and maintain your website or template implementation.
  • To send important service-related communications such as updates, changes, or security notices.
  • To improve our website, templates, and user experience.
  • To analyze usage trends and measure the effectiveness of our marketing.
  • To send you marketing communications where permitted by law (you can opt out at any time).
  • To prevent, detect, and address fraud, abuse, or security issues.
  • To comply with legal obligations and enforce our terms and policies.

Legal bases for processing (EEA/UK visitors)

If you are located in the European Economic Area or the United Kingdom, our legal bases for processing your Personal Data may include:

  • Performance of a contract (e.g. when we deliver services you have requested).
  • Legitimate interests (e.g. improving our services, securing our website, and communicating with you about relevant offerings).
  • Consent (e.g. for certain marketing activities or non-essential cookies).
  • Compliance with legal obligations (e.g. record-keeping, responding to lawful requests).

4. Cookies and Similar Technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies to provide and improve our website, personalize your experience, and analyze how our services are used.

What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They help the site recognize your device, remember your preferences, and understand how you interact with different pages.

Essential cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function and cannot be switched off in our systems. They are usually set only in response to actions you take, such as requesting pages or submitting forms.

Analytics cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors use our website so that we can measure and improve performance, such as which pages are popular and how users navigate between them.

Marketing cookies

Where used, these cookies may be set by us or our partners to build a profile of your interests and show you relevant content or advertisements on other sites. You can opt out where required by law.

Managing cookies

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings, including blocking or deleting them. If you choose to disable cookies, some parts of our website may not function properly.

Where required by applicable law, we will request your consent before using non-essential cookies or similar tracking technologies. You can withdraw your consent at any time by adjusting your browser settings or cookie preferences (where available).

5. Data Sharing and Retention

When we share your information

We do not sell your Personal Data. We may share your information with:

  • Service providers who help us operate our website, deliver our services, process payments, provide analytics, or send communications, under appropriate confidentiality and data protection obligations.
  • Professional advisors such as lawyers, accountants, or auditors where reasonably necessary.
  • Authorities or third parties where required by law, to protect our rights, or in connection with a business transaction (such as a merger, acquisition, or asset sale).

International transfers

If we transfer your Personal Data outside of the European Economic Area or United Kingdom, we will take appropriate steps to ensure that your information receives an adequate level of protection, such as by using standard contractual clauses or working with providers that implement equivalent safeguards.

Data retention

We keep your Personal Data only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law (for example, to comply with legal, accounting, or reporting obligations).

When we no longer need your Personal Data, we will delete it or anonymize it so that it can no longer be used to identify you.

6. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your location and applicable law, you may have certain rights regarding your Personal Data, including:

  • Access – to request a copy of the Personal Data we hold about you.
  • Rectification – to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Erasure – to request deletion of your Personal Data, where there is no good reason for us to continue processing it.
  • Restriction – to request that we restrict the processing of your Personal Data in certain circumstances.
  • Objection – to object to our processing of your Personal Data, particularly where we rely on legitimate interests or for direct marketing.
  • Data portability – to request that we transfer your Personal Data to you or another organization, where technically feasible.
  • Withdrawal of consent – where we rely on your consent, you may withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details in the “Contact Us” section below. We may need to verify your identity before responding to your request. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority if you believe that we have not handled your Personal Data in accordance with applicable law.

7. Data Security

We take reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your Personal Data against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure, or access.

However, no method of transmission over the internet or method of electronic storage is completely secure. While we strive to protect your Personal Data, we cannot guarantee absolute security. If you believe that your interaction with us is no longer secure, please contact us immediately.

8. Third-Party Websites and Services

Our website may contain links to third-party websites, services, or platforms (for example, template previews, social networks, payment providers, or external resources). We are not responsible for the privacy practices or the content of those third parties. If you follow a link to any third-party site, please review their privacy policies before submitting any Personal Data.

9. Children’s Privacy

Our website and services are intended for business users and are not directed to children under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect Personal Data from children. If you believe that a child has provided us with Personal Data, please contact us and we will take steps to delete such information where required by law.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technologies, legal requirements, or for other operational reasons. When we make material changes, we will take appropriate steps to inform you, such as by updating the “Last updated” date at the top of this page or providing a more prominent notice on our website.

We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically to stay informed about how we protect your information.

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, our data practices, or would like to exercise your rights, you can contact us at:

Email: info@heaventree.ie

When contacting us, please provide enough information to enable us to identify you and understand your request. We will respond as soon as reasonably possible and in accordance with applicable law.
